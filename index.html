OpenPP is a free, reusable, and standardized privacy policy.


Get started Learn more Contribute


Respects the user.

Privacy is important, which is why OpenPP prioritizes the user's rights. Using OpenPP as your project's privacy policy makes it clear that you respect your users' privacy.

Modular and flexible.

One privacy policy can't possibly cover all projects. That's why OpenPP is modular. With one base policy and multiple extensions that you can mix-and-match, you can be sure that OpenPP is the right fit.


Saves time.

Instead of including a privacy policy, many small projects simply operate without one. OpenPP makes it easy for small and medium sized projects to be transparent and responsible about how they use their users' data.

An open standard.

Instead of having to read a different privacy policy for every site, OpenPP standardizes privacy policies into recognizable IDs, like OPP:I (CO, PT, AL). Confused? Read on.



Let's build the perfect privacy policy.

For fine-grained control and flexibility, OpenPP policies have two parts: a single base policy and collection of clauses.



Which base policy fits your project?

The base policy gives the 'big picture' about the data you collect about your users, how it's used, and how it's shared.

Nothing — OPP:N

A simple and absolute base policy that respects the user's privacy.

Perfect for static websites, command line tools, and other simple software.

  • No data is collected about individual users.
  • Data is only collected in aggregate (for example, total pageviews or downloads).

Use OPP:A

Internal — OPP:I

A base policy that keeps all user data internal and out of the hands of third parties.

Perfect for all projects where the users' personal data isn't the product.

  • Users can expect their data to be collected.
  • Users' data isn't shared with any third parties.

Use OPP:I

Consent — OPP:C

A flexible base policy that keeps the user in control while still allowing data sharing.

Perfect for all other projects.

  • Users can expect their data to be collected.
  • A user's data isn't shared with any third parties without the user's consent.

Use OPP:C


Which clauses apply to your project?

Clauses allow you to be more specific about how you use, collect, process, and share your users' data. You can include any combination of clauses in your site's OpenPP policy.


Cookies

The project uses cookies to store information on the user's computer to retain data between sessions.

 Include this clause
OPP:* (CO, ...)

Passthrough

The project uses dependencies and extensions—like Disqus—which may have their own privacy policies that still apply.

 Include this clause
OPP:* (PT, ...)

Analytics

The project uses analytics software to track how it's used. The data collected may be either in aggregate or user-specific.

 Include this clause
OPP:* (AL, ...)

Anonymization

The project anonymizes user data in compliance the de-identification standard set forth in the EU General Data Protection Regulation.

 Include this clause
OPP:* (AN, ...)
